in its’ cold, monotonous voice.”Dammit Perry find out what’s wrong with this bucket of bolts ASAP!” barked Olsen at his
lead technician, “Meanwhile I’ll get the job done manually.” The two mechanics sent outside to repair
the solar panels could not find a way back in. They had been there to find out the fault behind the mysterious
power outage and stayed there since, their oxygen levels now reaching low levels and their lives currently at stake. Perry
routinely ran diagnostics and tests to locate and eliminate quirks in the system back when the two-year voyage began, but
lately his results proved inconclusive. Commander Olsen donned his spacesuit while assembling a team from the vessel’s
crew, preparing to rescue the stranded pair. He placed his hands on the emergency hatch- “I am afraid you cannot perform that action”
The whole crew were stunned because the computer never spoke unless it was spoken to, a basic rule for spaceship
supercomputers like itself. Everything clicked for Perry. The malfunctions, the power outage, the missing oxygen,
all of it was linked. The computer didn’t fail to comprehend or execute commands, it refused to.
“Computer, do you mind telling me what this is all about?” asked Olsen.
